Lamb Rogan Josh

Lamb Rogan Josh is a popular dish originating from the Indian subcontinent, specifically in the region of Kashmir. It is made with tender pieces of boneless lamb that are slow-cooked in a rich, aromatic sauce made from a blend of spices, tomatoes, and heavy cream. The key spices in a traditional Rogan Josh recipe include paprika, cumin, coriander, garam masala, cayenne pepper, turmeric, cardamom, and cinnamon. The result is a savory and flavorful dish that is both warming and comforting. It is typically served with rice or bread and is a popular main course option for special occasions and family dinners.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs boneless lamb, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 2 teaspoons paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 cup tomato puree
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large saucepan, heat the oil over medium heat. Add the onion and cook until soft and translucent, about 5-7 minutes.
  2. Add the garlic, ginger, paprika, cumin, coriander, garam masala, cayenne pepper, turmeric, cardamom, and cinnamon to the saucepan and stir to combine. Cook for 2-3 minutes, or until fragrant.
  3. Add the lamb to the saucepan and cook until browned, about 7-8 minutes. Stir in the tomato puree and water, and bring to a boil.
  4. Reduce the heat to low, cover the saucepan, and simmer until the lamb is tender, about 30-40 minutes. Stir in the heavy cream and season with salt and pepper, to taste.
  5. Serve the Lamb Rogan Josh hot, garnished with fresh cilantro and with rice or naan bread, if desired.
